Houseboats in Sausalito

A couple weeks ago, I went [kayaking in Sausalito](http://seatrek.com/) for the
day with my family and Gina. We wandered around the north bay, played tag with
sailboats and ferries (even if they didn't know it), made friends and enemies
with seals, and started absolutely no water fights at all. None. Not one!

One of the most interesting parts of the adventure was the
[houseboat community](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sausalito,_California#Houseboats)
on the northern edge of the town. Some were anchored out in the bay, but most
were in long term slips in dedicated houseboat marinas. The neighborhood was big
enough that you could actually call it a neighborhood, complete with streets and
intersections, an [HOA](http://www.floatinghomes.org/), and best of all, some
really fascinating architecture.

Most of the houseboats looked like normal houses, more or less, just with more
character and than your average landlocked house. As you'd expect, though, the
area
[has always attracted offbeat, artistic, quirky people](http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2003/10/04/HO30755.DTL&type=travel),
so plenty of the houseboats look just plain weird...which generally meant
_awesome_. The
[Taj Mahal](http://www.oursausalito.com/taj-mahal-houseboat.html) is probably
the most famous, but there were plenty of other
