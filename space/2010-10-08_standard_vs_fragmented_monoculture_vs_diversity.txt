Standards or diversity?


Of course, in practice things are rarely so black and white. As the classic joke
goes, "What's the best thing about standards? There are so many of them to
choose from!" Similarly, most monocultures aren't nearly as mono as they're
cracked up to be. Right now there are
[three _very_ different versions of Windows with significant market share](http://marketshare.hitslink.com/operating-system-market-share.aspx?qprid=10),
and that's before you take into account different service packs, patches,
and [versions of IE](http://marketshare.hitslink.com/browser-market-share.aspx?qprid=2).

Diversity isn't much better at staying diverse. Evolution and free markets may
support diversity, but they strongly favor winners. Mature industries often end
up with three major players with 50%, 35%, and 15% market share, but forces such
as
[emergent monopoly](http://marketshare.hitslink.com/search-engine-market-share.aspx?qprid=4)
and
[network effects](http://www.research-write.com/2010/02/social-networking-by-the-numbers.html)
can result in single players dominating heavily.
