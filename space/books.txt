Kurzweil is a technologist and scientist with an intimidating list of
accomplishments: voice recognition, voice synthesis, computer-generated art and
poetry, and some devastatingly accurate writing. He tends to make long-shot
predictions about how future technology will change our lives. He made 10 such
predictions in 1990, and 9 had come true by 2000. _Spiritual Machines_ contains
his predictions for 2010, 2020, and beyond.

  * [Thomas Friedman - Longitudes &
Attitudes](http://isbn.nu/cgi-bin/design-books?newform=yes&titlechoice=exact&authorchoice=exact&author=Thomas Friedman&title=Longitudes & Attitudes)
<nf>nf</nf>

Friedman is a giant of modern journalism. He already has three Pulitzers under
his belt, and he currently hangs his hat on the Foreign Affairs editor's desk at
the _New York Times_. This book is a collection of his columns post-9/11,
focusing on the relations between the Middle East, the US, and the rest of the
world. The format of the book lends itself to sound bites as opposed to
continuity, and he begins to repeat himself after about 100 pages. But those
first 100 have some startling insights.

  * [Lawrence Lessig - The Future of
Ideas](http://isbn.nu/cgi-bin/design-books?newform=yes&titlechoice=exact&authorchoice=exact&author=Lawrence Lessig&title=The Future of Ideas)
<nf>nf</nf>

Lessig was the Berkman Professor at Harvard Law School, and now he's at Stanford
(aw yeah...) He's currently one of the brightest minds in legal theory. This
book continues his exploration of law, communications, and the "tragedy of the
commons."

  * [Richard Dawkins - The Selfish
Gene](http://isbn.nu/cgi-bin/design-books?newform=yes&titlechoice=exact&authorchoice=exact&author=Richard Dawkins&title=The Selfish Gene)
<nf>nf</nf>

In the past 50 years, we've rapidly unraveled the mysteries of DNA, genetics,
and how evolution created the mind-boggling complexity of a human being. Dawkins
explains, and his style is a rare gem: just reading his writing makes you feel
smart.

  * [Matt Ridley -
Genome](http://isbn.nu/cgi-bin/design-books?newform=yes&titlechoice=exact&authorchoice=exact&author=Matt Ridley&title=Genome)
<nf>nf</nf>

The human genome is amazing. It's digital, it's immense, and it lays out the
instructions to build a complete, fully functioning human being. We've mapped
the entire thing, but we're only beginning to understand what to do with it.
Ridley takes you on a tour through all 23 chromosomes, what they do, and why you
should care.

  * [David Foster Wallace, A Supposedly Fun Thing I'll Never Do
Again](http://isbn.nu/cgi-bin/design-books?newform=yes&titlechoice=exact&authorchoice=exact&author=David Foster Wallace&title=A Supposedly Fun Thing I'll Never Do Again)
<nf>nf</nf>

This guy is just flat out hilarious. He's wickedly smart, and he spends just as
much time skewering state fairs and luxury cruise liners as he does
deconstructing David Lynch or contemporary American fiction. I bought this book
for E Unibus Pluram, which is only one of the seven essays, but they're all
incredible.
