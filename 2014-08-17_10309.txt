> we need to look at the society in which it grew. By the early 2040s, mass

> automation and globalisation had permanently raised the US unemployment rate to

> more then 30 percent, with 'underemployment' doubling that number. Enhanced

> social security and the glimmerings of a basic minimum income in some states had

> taken the financial sting out of this economic shift, but money alone couldn't

> replace the sense of meaning or direction that citizens had once derived from

> work - nor could it address the fragmentation of physical communities across

> the country.
